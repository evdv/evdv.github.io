Emotive or Affective Speech Synthesis is an active research area, with many different but similar sequence-to-sequence deep neural networks being developed continuously.
Despite this, the evaluation methods haven't received quite as much attention.
This is why I chose to focus on the impact of the amount of context and content available to a listener. In order to help with listening experiments, I also implemented a version of voice puppetry,  which works at inference time on NVIDIA's implementation of the FastPitch TTS model (v 1.1)   

Speech Synthesis can transform lives

Text To Speech Technology (i.e. Speech Synthesis) is a great tool to make any text more accessible. I've seen the effects of this myself, when a family member who is blind was able to use more products that "speak", such as a watch that literally tells the time, or a screen-reader app, so she can use a computer to send and receive emails.

One much-loved product is a proprietary audiobook device, which allows access to a whole world of unexplored stories.
The downside of audiobooks is that they can be expensive, because a voice talent needs to read every single sentence in a book.

TTS is not yet widely used to produce audio versions of books, because it is harder to synthesise speech that is not neutral, and that sounds as pleasant and appropriate for longer bodies of text as a voice talent.
That's where emotive TTS comes in!
